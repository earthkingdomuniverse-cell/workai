# WorkAI Launch Personas and Wedge

## Purpose

Define the first launch wedge and the primary personas for WorkAI 1.0.

## Primary wedge: Providers

The first wedge is the provider: a skilled person who wants to create offers, get discovered, send proposals, and close deals.

## Why providers first

| Reason | Detail |
| --- | --- |
| Supply driver | No providers means no offers and no marketplace inventory |
| Low-friction first action | Creating an offer is easier than waiting for perfect demand |
| AI pricing value | AI helps reduce pricing uncertainty |
| Clear value proposition | I have skills and want relevant work |
| Growth potential | Providers can invite requesters they already know |

## Provider pain points

- Not knowing what price to quote.
- Getting ignored after quoting.
- Depending on noisy groups or social posts for leads.
- Having skills but no trust/reviews yet.

## Provider first actions

1. Sign up.
2. Select provider role.
3. Complete profile.
4. Add skills.
5. Create first offer.
6. Use AI price guidance.
7. Browse matching requests.

## Persona 2: Requester

Requesters are founders, operators, creators, or small teams who need help but do not want to hire full-time or use a traditional agency.

### Requester pain points

- Not knowing who is good.
- Fear of scams or poor delivery.
- Not knowing fair budget.
- Needing help for a small or time-boxed task.

### Requester first actions

1. Sign up.
2. Select requester role.
3. Complete profile.
4. Browse offers.
5. Create first request.
6. Use AI match.
7. Review proposals.

## Persona 3: Operator/Admin

The operator/admin persona is internal and supports trust, safety, disputes, fraud, and marketplace quality.

### Operator triggers

- A dispute needs resolution.
- A high-risk signal appears.
- A fraud signal needs review.
- Reviews or operator decisions need moderation.

## Launch sequencing

### Phase 1: Seed supply

- Invite skilled providers.
- Target clear launch categories such as design, writing, video, and web development.
- Goal: 10-20 high-quality offers.

### Phase 2: Bootstrap demand

- Invite requesters with real needs.
- Goal: 10+ requests, 5+ proposals, and first deals.

### Phase 3: Controlled launch

- Expand to a wider early-access group.
- Goal: 50 offers, 30 requests, and 20+ proposal/deal events.

## Success criteria

| Metric | Target |
| --- | --- |
| Provider signups | 60%+ of first 100 users |
| Offer creation rate | 50%+ of providers create 1+ offer |
| First offer within 24h | 70%+ of activated providers |
| Request creation rate | 40%+ of requesters |
| Proposal acceptance | 20%+ accepted proposal rate |
| D7 retention | 40%+ |

## Risks

| Risk | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Providers do not create offers | AI price + first-action prompt |
| Providers get no deals | AI match and manual matching |
| Requesters do not create requests | Better explore and offer inventory |
| Ghost marketplace | Seed providers before broad demand launch |
